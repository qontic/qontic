Klein Tunneling 2D

Theory

A positive-energy 2D Dirac Gaussian packet starts on the left and hits a vertical scalar potential barrier.

\[ i\hbar\frac{\partial\psi}{\partial t} = \left[-i\hbar c\left(\sigma_x\partial_x+\sigma_y\partial_y\right) + mc^2\sigma_z + V(x)\right]\psi \]

The yellow dots are Bohmian positions in the 2D guidance field of the full spinor.

\[ \rho=\psi^\dagger\psi,\qquad \mathbf{j}=c\,\psi^\dagger(\sigma_x,\sigma_y)\psi,\qquad \dot{\mathbf{r}}=\frac{\mathbf{j}}{\rho}. \]

The amplitude view button only changes the background diagnostic: total spinor density or lower-component density. The particles still use the full Dirac spinor guidance field.

For incident total energy \(E\), the wall is in the Klein zone when \(V > E + mc^2\).
